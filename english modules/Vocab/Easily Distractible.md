### Formal Synonyms:

1. **Inattentive** – Lacking focus or concentration.
2. **Unfocused** – Unable to maintain attention on one thing.
3. **Distractible** – Prone to losing focus.
4. **Preoccupied** – Lost in thought or mentally elsewhere.
5. **Absent-minded** – Forgetful and unaware of surroundings.

### Informal/Casual Synonyms:

6. **Scatterbrained** – Frequently losing track of thoughts.
7. **Flighty** – Lacking consistency and easily distracted.
8. **Fidgety** – Restless and unable to stay focused.
9. **Zoned out** – Mentally disengaged or lost in thought.

### Stronger/More Negative Synonyms:

10. **Erratic** – Unpredictable and lacking focus.
11. **Frivolous** – Lacking seriousness or deep focus.
12. **Capricious** – Prone to changing attention suddenly.

### **Idioms**

1. **A wandering mind** – _His wandering mind makes it hard for him to focus during meetings._
2. **A short attention span** – _Kids these days have such a short attention span!_
3. **All over the place** – _His thoughts were all over the place during the presentation._
4. **Head in the clouds** – _She always has her head in the clouds instead of paying attention._
5. **Like a goldfish** – _He forgets what he's doing halfway through—he’s like a goldfish!_
6. **Easily led astray** – _He’s easily led astray whenever something new catches his eye._
7. **Can't stay on track** – _He starts a project but can’t stay on track for long._
8. **Lost in thought** – _She often gets lost in thought during important discussions._
9. **Jumps from one thing to another** – _He jumps from one hobby to another without finishing anything._
10. **Like a moth to a flame** – _He gets drawn into distractions like a moth to a flame._

### **Phrasal Verbs**

11. **Zone out** – _I totally zoned out during that long lecture._
12. **Space out** – _Sorry, I spaced out—what were you saying?_
13. **Drift off** – _She drifted off in the middle of the meeting._
14. **Lose track of (something)** – _He lost track of time while scrolling on his phone._
15. **Get sidetracked** – _I was working but got sidetracked by a funny video._
16. **Tune out** – _He tends to tune out when the topic doesn’t interest him._
17. **Go off on a tangent** – _She always goes off on a tangent instead of sticking to the point._
18. **Be easily thrown off** – _He’s easily thrown off whenever something unexpected happens._
19. **Trail off** – _Her explanation trailed off as she got distracted by something outside._
20. **Flip between (things)** – _He keeps flipping between tasks instead of finishing one first._
