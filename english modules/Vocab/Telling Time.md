### **Standard Time Expressions**

1. **O'clock** – _It's 3 o'clock._
2. **AM/PM** – _The meeting is at 9 AM._
3. **Minutes past** – _It's 10 minutes past 2._
4. **Minutes to** – _It's 20 minutes to 6._
5. **Quarter past** – _It's a quarter past 4._
6. **Quarter to** – _It's a quarter to 8._
7. **Half past** – _It's half past 5._

### **Informal Time Expressions**

1. **Around/about** – _I'll be there around 3._
2. **In the morning/afternoon/evening** – _I work in the afternoon._
3. **At night** – _I usually go for walks at night._
4. **At the crack of dawn** – _We left at the crack of dawn to catch the flight._
5. **Bright and early** – _I woke up bright and early for the meeting._

### **Idioms and Expressions**

1. **In no time** – _The task was completed in no time._
2. **Take your time** – _You don't need to rush, take your time._
3. **Time flies** – _Time flies when you're having fun._
4. **Kill time** – _I read a book to kill time while waiting._
5. **All in good time** – _Don't worry, everything will be done all in good time._
6. **The eleventh hour** – _He finished the project at the eleventh hour._
7. **A stitch in time saves nine** – _Fix the problem now, a stitch in time saves nine._
8. **Time is of the essence** – _We need to act quickly; time is of the essence._
9. **Pressing for time** – _Sorry, I’m pressing for time and can't stay longer._
10. **A matter of time** – _It’s only a matter of time before he finds out._
    

### **Phrasal Verbs**

1. **Run out of time** – _We ran out of time before finishing the test._
2. **Catch up on (time)** – _I'll catch up on time after this meeting._
3. **Set back** – _The delay set us back an hour._
4. **Pass by** – _The years pass by quickly._
5. **Time out** – _We need a time out to discuss this._
6. **Clock in/out** – _I clock in at 9 AM and clock out at 5 PM._
7. **Run late** – _Sorry I’m running late, I’ll be there in 10 minutes._

### **More Specific Time References**

1. **At the moment** – _I'm busy at the moment, can I call you back?_
2. **At once** – _You need to do it at once!_
3. **Before long** – _We should reach the station before long._
4. **In due course** – _The results will be announced in due course._
5. **In the nick of time** – _He arrived in the nick of time to catch the train._
6. **In a while** – _I’ll call you in a while._
7. **Right on time** – _She arrived right on time for the meeting._
    

### **Military and 24-hour Time**

1. **Zero hundred hours (00:00)** – _The flight departs at zero hundred hours._
2. **Nineteen hundred hours (19:00)** – _The event starts at nineteen hundred hours._
3. **Twenty-one hundred hours (21:00)** – _The show begins at twenty-one hundred hours._
    

### **Expressions for Time Intervals**

1. **In the long run** – _In the long run, it will pay off._
2. **In the short term** – _We need to focus on the short term goals._
3. **For the time being** – _For the time being, let's focus on this task._
4. **From time to time** – _I visit the old house from time to time._
5. **In a heartbeat** – _I’d do it in a heartbeat if I had the chance._
6. **At times** – _At times, I feel overwhelmed by the workload._
7. **On the dot** – _He arrived at 3 o'clock on the dot._
    

### **Time-Related Slang/Colloquial Phrases**

1. **Five o'clock shadow** – _He had a five o'clock shadow after a long day._
2. **All-nighter** – _I pulled an all-nighter to finish the project._
3. **Early bird** – _She’s an early bird and wakes up at 5 AM._
4. **Night owl** – _He’s a night owl and stays up until late._
5. **Crack of dawn** – _We left at the crack of dawn to avoid traffic._
