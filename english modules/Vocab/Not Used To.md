### **Ways to Say You’re Not Used to Something in English**

When expressing that something is unfamiliar or difficult due to a lack of experience, you can use different **expressions, idioms, phrases, and phrasal verbs**. Below is a categorized and sorted list of ways to say that you are not used to something.

---

### **1. Common Phrases** (Most Natural & Frequently Used)

- _I’m not used to [something]._ → _I’m not used to waking up early._
    
- _I haven’t gotten used to [something] yet._ → _I haven’t gotten used to this new job yet._
    
- _This is new to me._ → _Living alone is new to me._
    
- _I’m still adjusting to [something]._ → _I’m still adjusting to life in a new country._
    
- _I’m not familiar with [something]._ → _I’m not familiar with this software._
    
- _I’m struggling to get used to [something]._ → _I’m struggling to get used to speaking in front of a crowd._
    
- _I find it hard to [do something]._ → _I find it hard to work at night._
    

---

### **2. Idioms** (More Figurative)

- _It’s like learning to walk all over again._ → _(Used when relearning or adapting to something difficult.)_
    
- _It’s a whole new ballgame for me._ → _(Something is completely different from what you're used to.)_
    
- _I feel like a fish out of water._ → _(I feel uncomfortable and out of place.)_
    
- _I’m out of my element._ → _(I’m in a situation I’m not familiar with.)_
    
- _This is uncharted territory for me._ → _(I have no experience with this.)_
    
- _I’m still finding my feet._ → _(I’m still learning how to manage in this new situation.)_
    

---

### **3. Phrasal Verbs** (More Informal & Conversational)

- _Get used to_ → _I need to get used to waking up early._
    
- _Ease into [something]_ → _I’m easing into my new routine._
    
- _Adapt to [something]_ → _I’m adapting to city life._
    
- _Settle into [something]_ → _I’m settling into my new role at work._
    
- _Break into [something]_ → _It’s hard to break into the habit of exercising daily._
    
- _Get the hang of [something]_ → _I haven’t gotten the hang of driving yet._
    
- _Shake off the unfamiliarity_ → _It took me a while to shake off the unfamiliarity of the new office._
    

---

### **4. More Formal/Polite Expressions**

- _I’m in the process of adapting to [something]._ → _(More professional and neutral.)_
    
- _I haven’t fully acclimated to [something] yet._ → _(Used in a more formal context.)_
    
- _This requires an adjustment period for me._ → _(Used in workplaces or formal discussions.)_
    
- _I’m gradually becoming accustomed to [something]._ → _(Sounds more refined and structured.)_
