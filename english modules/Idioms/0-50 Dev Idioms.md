Вот 50 популярных идиом, которые часто встречаются в разговоре разработчиков:

---

### **1. Идиомы о работе в IT и разработке**

1. **Hit the ground running** – начать быстро и эффективно  
    _"Our new developer hit the ground running and fixed several bugs on his first day!"_
    
2. **Think outside the box** – мыслить нестандартно  
    _"We need to think outside the box to solve this UI issue."_
    
3. **Burn the midnight oil** – работать до поздней ночи  
    _"We had to burn the midnight oil to meet the deadline."_
    
4. **Back to the drawing board** – начать заново  
    _"This feature isn’t working as expected. Let’s go back to the drawing board."_
    
5. **Keep (someone) in the loop** – держать в курсе  
    _"Please keep me in the loop about the API changes."_
    
6. **Pull the plug** – прекратить работу над чем-то  
    _"The client pulled the plug on the project due to budget cuts."_
    
7. **A learning curve** – кривая обучения (процесс привыкания к новому)  
    _"There’s a steep learning curve with this framework."_
    
8. **Crash and burn** – полный провал  
    _"Our last deployment crashed and burned due to a database issue."_
    
9. **Not reinvent the wheel** – не изобретать велосипед  
    _"We can use an existing library; there’s no need to reinvent the wheel."_
    
10. **A quick and dirty solution** – быстрое, но некачественное решение  
    _"I wrote a quick and dirty fix, but we need to refactor it later."_
    

---

### **2. Идиомы о кодинге и отладке**

11. **Bite the bullet** – смириться с чем-то сложным  
    _"I don’t like working with legacy code, but I have to bite the bullet."_
    
12. **On the same page** – понимать друг друга  
    _"Let’s have a meeting to make sure we’re on the same page."_
    
13. **Cut corners** – халтурить, делать кое-как  
    _"We can’t cut corners when it comes to security."_
    
14. **Throw in the towel** – сдаться  
    _"After debugging for five hours, I wanted to throw in the towel."_
    
15. **In hot water** – иметь проблемы  
    _"I forgot to test the code, and now I’m in hot water with my manager."_
    
16. **Out of the woods** – выйти из сложной ситуации  
    _"We finally fixed the memory leak. We’re not out of the woods yet, but it’s progress."_
    
17. **Put on the back burner** – отложить на потом  
    _"Let’s put this feature on the back burner for now."_
    
18. **Buggy as hell** – крайне нестабильный код  
    _"This old JavaScript module is buggy as hell."_
    
19. **Rubber duck debugging** – объяснять проблему, чтобы понять её  
    _"I was rubber duck debugging with a colleague, and I found the issue myself."_
    
20. **Code smell** – подозрительный код  
    _"This function does too many things; it’s a code smell."_
    

---

### **3. Идиомы о командной работе и управлении проектами**

21. **A bottleneck** – узкое место, замедляющее процесс  
    _"Our slow database queries are a major bottleneck."_
    
22. **Push the envelope** – выйти за пределы возможного  
    _"We’re pushing the envelope with this AI-powered feature."_
    
23. **Throw under the bus** – подставить кого-то  
    _"The manager threw me under the bus for the failed release."_
    
24. **Game changer** – что-то кардинально меняющее ситуацию  
    _"This new framework is a game changer for frontend development."_
    
25. **Hit a roadblock** – столкнуться с препятствием  
    _"We hit a roadblock while integrating the payment system."_
    
26. **Drop the ball** – допустить ошибку  
    _"I really dropped the ball by forgetting to review the PR."_
    
27. **The elephant in the room** – очевидная, но игнорируемая проблема  
    _"The tight deadline is the elephant in the room nobody wants to discuss."_
    
28. **Call the shots** – принимать решения  
    _"As the lead developer, he calls the shots on architecture decisions."_
    
29. **A moving target** – постоянно меняющаяся задача  
    _"The client keeps changing the requirements, it’s a moving target."_
    
30. **The ball is in your court** – теперь дело за тобой  
    _"I fixed the merge conflicts; the ball is in your court now."_
    

---

### **4. Идиомы о технологиях и индустрии**

31. **State of the art** – передовой, современный  
    _"This is a state-of-the-art cloud solution."_
    
32. **Bells and whistles** – ненужные «фишки»  
    _"We don’t need all these bells and whistles in the UI."_
    
33. **Dogfooding** – тестировать свой же продукт  
    _"We need to start dogfooding our own app."_
    
34. **Garbage in, garbage out** – плохие данные = плохие результаты  
    _"If the input is wrong, the output will be wrong too – garbage in, garbage out."_
    
35. **The Holy Grail** – нечто очень ценное и редкое  
    _"A fast and scalable frontend framework is the Holy Grail."_
    
36. **A silver bullet** – универсальное решение  
    _"There’s no silver bullet for optimizing performance."_
    
37. **Riding the wave** – идти в ногу с трендами  
    _"We're riding the wave of AI development."_
    
38. **Plug and play** – легко настраиваемый  
    _"This API is plug and play, no configuration needed."_
    
39. **Black box** – непрозрачная система  
    _"This AI model is a black box; we don’t know how it makes decisions."_
    
40. **Eating our own dog food** – использовать свой продукт  
    _"Let’s start eating our own dog food and test the app internally."_
    

---

### **5. Разное**

41. **Throw money at (something)** – решать проблему деньгами  
    _"You can’t just throw money at bad code and expect it to work."_
    
42. **A needle in a haystack** – что-то труднонаходимое  
    _"Finding that bug was like looking for a needle in a haystack."_
    
43. **By the book** – строго по правилам  
    _"The security team wants everything done by the book."_
    
44. **A shot in the dark** – догадка, риск  
    _"Trying this fix is just a shot in the dark, but it might work."_
    
45. **Go the extra mile** – сделать больше, чем требуется  
    _"He went the extra mile to optimize the performance."_
    
46. **A gut feeling** – интуиция  
    _"I have a gut feeling that this bug is related to the database."_
    
47. **Out of the blue** – неожиданно  
    _"The CEO joined our stand-up out of the blue."_
    
48. **Put your money where your mouth is** – подкреплять слова делами  
    _"If you think this framework is better, put your money where your mouth is and build something with it."_
    
49. **Read between the lines** – понимать скрытый смысл  
    _"Reading between the lines, I think they want to cancel the project."_
    
50. **Up and running** – успешно запущено  
    _"The new feature is up and running."_
