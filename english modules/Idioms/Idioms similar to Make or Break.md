### 1. **Make or Break**  

- **Meaning**: A situation that will result in either **great success or total failure**.  
- **Example**: *"The final exam is a **make or break** moment for my scholarship."*  

### 2. **Do or Die**  

- **Meaning**: A critical situation where **you must succeed or face serious consequences**.  
-  **Example**: *"It’s **do or die**—if we lose this game, we’re out of the tournament."*  

### 3. **Sink or Swim**  

- **Meaning**: To either **succeed by your own efforts or fail completely**.  
- **Example**: *"The new interns were thrown into the project—it’s **sink or swim** for them."*  

### 4. **Now or Never**  

- **Meaning**: A final chance to do something.  
- **Example**: *"If you want to confess your feelings, it’s **now or never**!"*  

### 5. **All or Nothing**  

- **Meaning**: Taking a **big risk** where you either win everything or lose everything.  
- **Example**: *"He bet his entire savings—it’s an **all or nothing** gamble."*  

### 6. **Put Up or Shut Up**  

- **Meaning**: **Prove your ability** or stop talking about it.  
- **Example**: *"You keep saying you can beat me—**put up or shut up**!"*  

### 7. **Cross That Bridge When You Come to It**  

- **Meaning**: Deal with a problem **only when it happens**, not before.  
- **Example**: *"Don’t stress about the interview yet—**cross that bridge when you come to it**."*  

### 8. **The Last Straw**  

- **Meaning**: The final problem that makes a situation **unbearable**.  
- **Example**: *"When he forgot our anniversary, that was **the last straw**—I’m leaving him."*  

### 9. **Hit or Miss**  

- **Meaning**: Something that is **unpredictable** (could succeed or fail randomly).  
- **Example**: *"His jokes are **hit or miss**—sometimes funny, sometimes awkward."*  

### 10. **Go Big or Go Home**  

- **Meaning**: If you’re going to do something, **do it boldly** or don’t bother.  
- **Example**: *"We’re renting a limo for prom—**go big or go home**!"*  

### 11. **Burn Your Bridges**  

- **Meaning**: Do something that **makes return impossible** (usually negative).  
- **Example**: *"Yelling at your boss was a mistake—you really **burned your bridges**."*  

### 12. **Bite the Bullet**  

- **Meaning**: **Endure a painful situation** bravely.  
- **Example**: *"I hate the dentist, but I’ll just have to **bite the bullet**."*  

### 13. **Throw in the Towel**  

- **Meaning**: **Give up** or admit defeat.  
- **Example**: *"After failing three times, he **threw in the towel** on his startup."*  

### 14. **Play It by Ear**  

- **Meaning**: Decide **spontaneously** as things happen.  
- **Example**: *"We don’t have a plan—let’s just **play it by ear**."*  

### 15. **Jump on the Bandwagon**  

- **Meaning**: Join a popular trend.  
- **Example**: *"After Bitcoin boomed, everyone **jumped on the bandwagon**."*  

### 16. **Cut to the Chase**  

- **Meaning**: **Skip the details** and get to the main point.  
- **Example**: *"Stop rambling—**cut to the chase**!"*  

### 17. **Spill the Beans**  

- **Meaning**: **Reveal a secret** accidentally or intentionally.  
- **Example**: *"She **spilled the beans** about the surprise party."*  

### 18. **Pull Someone’s Leg**  

- **Meaning**: **Joke** or tease someone playfully.  
- **Example**: *"Relax, I’m just **pulling your leg**—I didn’t really crash your car."*  

### 19. **Hit the Nail on the Head**  

- **Meaning**: Describe something **exactly right**.  
- **Example**: *"You **hit the nail on the head**—that’s exactly why I’m upset."*  

### 20. **Kick the Bucket**  

- **Meaning**: A humorous/slang way to say **"die."**  
- **Example**: *"My old laptop finally **kicked the bucket**."*
