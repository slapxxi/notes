### 1. **Make or Break**

- **Meaning**: A situation that will result in either **great success or total failure**.
- **Example**: _"The final exam is a **make or break** moment for my scholarship."_

### 2. **Do or Die**

- **Meaning**: A critical situation where **you must succeed or face serious consequences**.
- **Example**: _"It’s **do or die**—if we lose this game, we’re out of the tournament."_

### 3. **Sink or Swim**

- **Meaning**: To either **succeed by your own efforts or fail completely**.
- **Example**: _"The new interns were thrown into the project—it’s **sink or swim** for them."_

### 4. **Now or Never**

- **Meaning**: A final chance to do something.
- **Example**: _"If you want to confess your feelings, it’s **now or never**!"_

### 5. **All or Nothing**

- **Meaning**: Taking a **big risk** where you either win everything or lose everything.
- **Example**: _"He bet his entire savings—it’s an **all or nothing** gamble."_

### 6. **Put Up or Shut Up**

- **Meaning**: **Prove your ability** or stop talking about it.
- **Example**: _"You keep saying you can beat me—**put up or shut up**!"_

### 7. **Cross That Bridge When You Come to It**

- **Meaning**: Deal with a problem **only when it happens**, not before.
- **Example**: _"Don’t stress about the interview yet—**cross that bridge when you come to it**."_

### 8. **The Last Straw**

- **Meaning**: The final problem that makes a situation **unbearable**.
- **Example**: _"When he forgot our anniversary, that was **the last straw**—I’m leaving him."_

### 9. **Hit or Miss**

- **Meaning**: Something that is **unpredictable** (could succeed or fail randomly).
- **Example**: _"His jokes are **hit or miss**—sometimes funny, sometimes awkward."_

### 10. **Go Big or Go Home**

- **Meaning**: If you’re going to do something, **do it boldly** or don’t bother.
- **Example**: _"We’re renting a limo for prom—**go big or go home**!"_

### 11. **Burn Your Bridges**

- **Meaning**: Do something that **makes return impossible** (usually negative).
- **Example**: _"Yelling at your boss was a mistake—you really **burned your bridges**."_

### 12. **Bite the Bullet**

- **Meaning**: **Endure a painful situation** bravely.
- **Example**: _"I hate the dentist, but I’ll just have to **bite the bullet**."_

### 13. **Throw in the Towel**

- **Meaning**: **Give up** or admit defeat.
- **Example**: _"After failing three times, he **threw in the towel** on his startup."_

### 14. **Play It by Ear**

- **Meaning**: Decide **spontaneously** as things happen.
- **Example**: _"We don’t have a plan—let’s just **play it by ear**."_

### 15. **Jump on the Bandwagon**

- **Meaning**: Join a popular trend.
- **Example**: _"After Bitcoin boomed, everyone **jumped on the bandwagon**."_

### 16. **Cut to the Chase**

- **Meaning**: **Skip the details** and get to the main point.
- **Example**: _"Stop rambling—**cut to the chase**!"_

### 17. **Spill the Beans**

- **Meaning**: **Reveal a secret** accidentally or intentionally.
- **Example**: _"She **spilled the beans** about the surprise party."_

### 18. **Pull Someone’s Leg**

- **Meaning**: **Joke** or tease someone playfully.
- **Example**: _"Relax, I’m just **pulling your leg**—I didn’t really crash your car."_

### 19. **Hit the Nail on the Head**

- **Meaning**: Describe something **exactly right**.
- **Example**: _"You **hit the nail on the head**—that’s exactly why I’m upset."_

### 20. **Kick the Bucket**

- **Meaning**: A humorous/slang way to say **"die."**
- **Example**: _"My old laptop finally **kicked the bucket**."_
