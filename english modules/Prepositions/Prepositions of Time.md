### **Comprehensive List of Prepositions of Time with Examples**

Prepositions of time indicate **when** something happens in relation to another time event. They help answer the question **"When?"** and are essential for both everyday conversation and professional communication.

---

## **1. Common Prepositions of Time**

| **Preposition** | **Usage**                                                  | **Example Sentence**                                                |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **In**          | Used for **months, years, centuries, long periods**        | _I was born **in** July._ / _She lived in London **in** 2020._      |
| **On**          | Used for **specific days, dates, special occasions**       | _The meeting is **on** Monday._ / _We met **on** Christmas Eve._    |
| **At**          | Used for **specific times, holidays, and precise moments** | _The train leaves **at** 5 PM._ / _I will see you **at** midnight._ |

---

## **2. Less Common but Useful Prepositions of Time**

| **Preposition**  | **Usage**                                                 | **Example Sentence**                            |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **By**           | **Before or at a specific time**                          | _I will finish the report **by** Friday._       |
| **Before**       | **Earlier than a certain time**                           | _She arrived **before** 8 AM._                  |
| **After**        | **Later than a certain time**                             | _Let's meet **after** lunch._                   |
| **During**       | **Within a time period**                                  | _No phones are allowed **during** the exam._    |
| **Until (Till)** | **Up to a specific time**                                 | _The store is open **until** 10 PM._            |
| **From... to**   | **Starting and ending time**                              | _The office is open **from** 9 AM **to** 6 PM._ |
| **Since**        | **Starting point of an action continuing to the present** | _I've lived here **since** 2015._               |
| **For**          | **Duration of time**                                      | _I have worked here **for** five years._        |

---

## **3. Advanced Prepositions of Time**

| **Preposition** | **Usage**                         | **Example Sentence**                          |
| --------------- | --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **Throughout**  | **From the beginning to the end** | _It rained **throughout** the night._         |
| **Within**      | **Inside a time frame**           | _Your order will arrive **within** two days._ |
| **Over**        | **During a certain period**       | _We discussed the project **over** dinner._   |
| **Beyond**      | **After a specific time**         | _We won’t work **beyond** 6 PM._              |
| **Up to**       | **Until a point in time**         | _He worked there **up to** his retirement._   |

---

## **4. How to Incorporate Prepositions of Time into Speech**

### **A. Talking About Schedules**

- **"The class starts at 8 AM and ends at 10 AM."**
- **"We have a holiday on New Year's Day."**
- **"The project must be submitted by Friday."**

### **B. Describing Duration**

- **"I've lived here since 2020."**
- **"I studied for three hours."**
- **"She traveled throughout the summer."**

### **C. Giving Deadlines and Appointments**

- **"I'll be back within an hour."**
- **"We’ll meet after lunch."**
- **"The store is open until 9 PM."**
