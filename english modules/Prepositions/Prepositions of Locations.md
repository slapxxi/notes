### **Comprehensive List of Prepositions of Location with Examples**

Prepositions of location describe the **position** or **place** of something relative to another object. They help answer the question **“Where?”** and are commonly used in both everyday speech and professional communication.

---

## **1. Common Prepositions of Location**

|**Preposition**|**Meaning**|**Example Sentence**|
|---|---|---|
|**In**|Inside an enclosed space|_The books are **in** the bag._|
|**On**|Resting on a surface|_The phone is **on** the table._|
|**At**|A specific point or place|_He is **at** the door._|
|**By**|Near something|_The café is **by** the bank._|
|**Next to**|Immediately beside|_She sat **next to** me._|
|**Beside**|At the side of|_The lamp is **beside** the bed._|
|**Near**|Close to but not touching|_The school is **near** the park._|
|**Between**|In the middle of two things|_The shop is **between** the bank and the pharmacy._|
|**Behind**|At the back of something|_The car is parked **behind** the building._|
|**In front of**|Facing or before something|_She stood **in front of** the mirror._|
|**Above**|Higher than something|_The clock is **above** the door._|
|**Over**|Directly above, sometimes covering|_A bridge goes **over** the river._|
|**Below**|Lower than something|_The temperature is **below** freezing._|
|**Under**|Directly beneath|_The keys are **under** the pillow._|

---

## **2. Less Common but Useful Prepositions of Location**

|**Preposition**|**Meaning**|**Example Sentence**|
|---|---|---|
|**Underneath**|Directly below, sometimes hidden|_The cat is **underneath** the couch._|
|**Beneath**|Positioned lower than something|_The water is **beneath** the bridge._|
|**Along**|Following the length of something|_We walked **along** the beach._|
|**Among**|In the middle of multiple things|_He was sitting **among** his friends._|
|**Amid**|Surrounded by|_The cabin is **amid** tall trees._|
|**Inside**|Within a closed space|_The papers are **inside** the folder._|
|**Outside**|On the exterior|_The car is parked **outside** the house._|
|**Onto**|Moving towards a surface|_He jumped **onto** the chair._|
|**Into**|Moving inside something|_She walked **into** the room._|
|**Out of**|Moving from inside to outside|_He stepped **out of** the car._|

---

## **3. Advanced Prepositions of Location**

|**Preposition**|**Meaning**|**Example Sentence**|
|---|---|---|
|**Opposite**|Facing directly|_The bank is **opposite** the post office._|
|**Against**|Touching or leaning on|_He leaned **against** the wall._|
|**Perched on**|Positioned on top of something|_The bird is **perched on** the branch._|
|**Within**|Inside a boundary|_The castle is **within** the forest._|
|**Beyond**|Further than a certain point|_The mountains are **beyond** the river._|
|**Underneath**|Beneath something|_The files are **underneath** the books._|

---

## **4. How to Incorporate Prepositions of Location into Speech**

### **A. Giving Directions**

- **"The pharmacy is next to the supermarket."**
    
- **"Go past the bank, and you'll see the post office on your left."**
    
- **"Walk along the street until you reach the park."**
    

### **B. Describing Object Locations**

- **"My phone is on the desk."**
    
- **"The keys are under the mat."**
    
- **"The restaurant is opposite the cinema."**
    

### **C. Talking About People’s Positions**

- **"She is sitting beside her friend."**
    
- **"He is standing behind me in line."**
    
- **"They are waiting at the entrance."**
