Here’s a list of 50 commonly used prepositions of **cause** along with usage examples:

### 1. **Because of**

- _The game was canceled **because of** the rain._

### 2. **Due to**

- _The flight was delayed **due to** technical issues._

### 3. **Thanks to**

- _We finished the project on time **thanks to** her hard work._

### 4. **Owing to**

- _The event was postponed **owing to** unforeseen circumstances._

### 5. **As a result of**

- _The roads are icy **as a result of** the snowfall._

### 6. **On account of**

- _The meeting was rescheduled **on account of** a family emergency._

### 7. **In view of**

- _In view of the recent changes, we need to adjust our plan._

### 8. **In consequence of**

- _In consequence of the bad weather, the roads were closed._

### 9. **By reason of**

- _She was absent **by reason of** illness._

### 10. **For the sake of**

- _He did all of this **for the sake of** his children._

### 11. **As a consequence of**

- _The company suffered **as a consequence of** poor management._

### 12. **In light of**

- _In light of recent developments, we need to make a decision quickly._

### 13. **For**

- _They were punished **for** breaking the rules._

### 14. **Out of**

- _She quit her job **out of** frustration._

### 15. **On the grounds of**

- _He was dismissed **on the grounds of** poor performance._

### 16. **By virtue of**

- _He won the prize **by virtue of** his excellent performance._

### 17. **As a result**

- _He failed the exam **as a result** of not studying enough._

### 18. **In consequence**

- _In consequence of the delay, we missed the train._

### 19. **In response to**

- _The company issued a statement **in response to** the criticism._

### 20. **Because**

- _She was late **because** of heavy traffic._

### 21. **Considering**

- _Considering the circumstances, I think we should reschedule._

### 22. **In the wake of**

- _In the wake of the accident, the road was closed for several hours._

### 23. **For fear of**

- _He stayed home **for fear of** getting sick._

### 24. **In order to**

- _She worked hard **in order to** pass the exam._

### 25. **As a means of**

- _He used his savings **as a means of** starting his own business._

### 26. **In the interest of**

### 27. **In anticipation of**

### 28. **For the purpose of**

### 36. **For want of**

### 37. **At the expense of**

### 38. **In consideration of**

### 39. **With a view to**

### 40. **With the intention of**

### 42. **In the course of**

### 45. **In the hope of**

- _They invested a lot of money **in the hope of** making a profit._

- _He achieved his goals **through** hard work and perseverance._

### 47. **With respect to**

### 48. **In connection with**

- _The company grew rapidly **on the back of** successful marketing._
