### **Comprehensive List of Prepositions of Direction/Movement with Examples**

Prepositions of **direction/movement** indicate movement from one place to another. They answer the question **"Where to?"** and are essential in describing movement, travel, and spatial relationships.

---

## **1. Common Prepositions of Direction/Movement**

| **Preposition** | **Usage**                               | **Example Sentence**                    |
| --------------- | --------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **To**          | Movement toward a specific destination  | _She went **to** the store._            |
| **Into**        | Movement from outside to inside         | _He walked **into** the room._          |
| **Onto**        | Movement toward a surface               | _The cat jumped **onto** the table._    |
| **Out of**      | Movement from inside to outside         | _She ran **out of** the house._         |
| **Off**         | Movement away from a surface            | _He stepped **off** the bus._           |
| **Onto**        | Movement toward and ending on a surface | _The dog jumped **onto** the bed._      |
| **From**        | The starting point of movement          | _They traveled **from** Paris to Rome._ |

---

## **2. Less Common but Useful Prepositions of Direction/Movement**

| **Preposition**      | **Usage**                                 | **Example Sentence**                       |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Toward (Towards)** | Movement in the direction of something    | _She ran **toward** the exit._             |
| **Through**          | Movement from one side to the other       | _The train passed **through** the tunnel._ |
| **Over**             | Movement above something without touching | _The plane flew **over** the mountains._   |
| **Under**            | Movement beneath something                | _He crawled **under** the table._          |
| **Past**             | Movement beyond a location                | _We walked **past** the library._          |
| **Along**            | Movement following a line or path         | _She walked **along** the riverbank._      |
| **Across**           | Movement from one side to the other       | _He swam **across** the lake._             |

---

## **3. Advanced Prepositions of Direction/Movement**

| **Preposition** | **Usage**                        | **Example Sentence**                   |
| --------------- | -------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **Up**          | Movement to a higher place       | _She climbed **up** the stairs._       |
| **Down**        | Movement to a lower place        | _He ran **down** the hill._            |
| **Beyond**      | Movement past a certain point    | _We drove **beyond** the city limits._ |
| **Around**      | Movement in a circular direction | _He walked **around** the park._       |
| **Between**     | Movement involving two points    | _She squeezed **between** the chairs._ |
| **About**       | Movement in no fixed direction   | _He wandered **about** the streets._   |

---

## **4. How to Incorporate Prepositions of Direction/Movement into Speech**

### **A. Giving Directions**

- **"Go straight and turn left at the intersection."**
- **"Walk through the park to get to my house."**
- **"Drive past the mall and you'll see the restaurant on your right."**

### **B. Describing Travel and Movement**

- **"She moved to another city last year."**
- **"They ran into the house to escape the rain."**
- **"He threw the ball over the fence."**

### **C. Talking About Daily Actions**

- **"I walked up the stairs to my office."**
- **"She looked out of the window."**
- **"He put his bag onto the chair."**
