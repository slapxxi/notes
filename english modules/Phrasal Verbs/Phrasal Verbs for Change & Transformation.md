Here’s a **comprehensive list of 50 most popular phrasal verbs for change and transformation**, sorted by popularity, with examples of usage:

---

### **1. Change into**

- **Meaning**: To transform or become something else.
    
- **Example**: _She changed into her workout clothes before going to the gym._
    

### **2. Turn into**

- **Meaning**: To become something different or transform into something.
    
- **Example**: _The rain turned into snow by the evening._
    

### **3. Break down**

- **Meaning**: To collapse or fail, often used for systems or processes.
    
- **Example**: _The car broke down in the middle of the road._
    

### **4. Grow into**

- **Meaning**: To develop into something over time.
    
- **Example**: _He grew into a confident leader over the years._
    

### **5. Switch to**

- **Meaning**: To change from one thing to another.
    
- **Example**: _I decided to switch to a new phone provider for better service._
    

### **6. Bring about**

- **Meaning**: To cause something to happen or lead to a change.
    
- **Example**: _The new law brought about a significant change in the healthcare system._
    

### **7. Phase out**

- **Meaning**: To gradually stop using or doing something.
    
- **Example**: _The company is phasing out the old software in favor of the new version._
    

### **8. Turn around**

- **Meaning**: To improve or change something for the better.
    
- **Example**: _The company was struggling, but a new management team turned it around._
    

### **9. Come about**

- **Meaning**: To happen or occur, often unexpectedly.
    
- **Example**: _The opportunity to work abroad came about when a colleague left the company._
    

### **10. Break up**

- **Meaning**: To end a relationship or an organization.
    
- **Example**: _They decided to break up after five years together._
    

### **11. Grow out of**

- **Meaning**: To mature beyond something, or stop needing something.
    
- **Example**: _He grew out of his childhood fears and became more confident._
    

### **12. Bring up**

- **Meaning**: To raise a topic or issue for discussion.
    
- **Example**: _She brought up the issue of budget cuts during the meeting._
    

### **13. Settle down**

- **Meaning**: To stabilize or become less active, often after a change.
    
- **Example**: _After years of traveling, he decided to settle down in one place._
    

### **14. Fall apart**

- **Meaning**: To break into pieces or deteriorate, often emotionally or structurally.
    
- **Example**: _Their relationship fell apart after years of misunderstandings._
    

### **15. Put off**

- **Meaning**: To delay or postpone something.
    
- **Example**: _I put off the decision until I had more information._
    

### **16. Clean up**

- **Meaning**: To tidy or improve something by removing or correcting mistakes.
    
- **Example**: _The new manager cleaned up the department by reorganizing the workflow._
    

### **17. Turn up**

- **Meaning**: To appear or show up unexpectedly, often after a change in circumstances.
    
- **Example**: _She turned up at the party after being invited last minute._
    

### **18. Get rid of**

- **Meaning**: To remove or dispose of something, often to bring change.
    
- **Example**: _We need to get rid of the outdated equipment before the new ones arrive._
    

### **19. Step up**

- **Meaning**: To increase efforts or improve performance.
    
- **Example**: _The team stepped up their efforts after receiving feedback._
    

### **20. Change over**

- **Meaning**: To switch from one system, method, or thing to another.
    
- **Example**: _The company changed over to a new accounting system this year._
    

### **21. Work out**

- **Meaning**: To resolve or figure out a problem or situation.
    
- **Example**: _We need to work out a plan to address the recent changes._
    

### **22. Come up with**

- **Meaning**: To think of or create an idea, solution, or plan.
    
- **Example**: _He came up with a great idea to improve customer service._
    

### **23. Step down**

- **Meaning**: To resign or leave a position of responsibility.
    
- **Example**: _The CEO stepped down after ten years at the company._
    

### **24. Get through**

- **Meaning**: To successfully finish or pass through something difficult.
    
- **Example**: _She got through the tough times and emerged stronger._
    

### **25. Put back**

- **Meaning**: To return something to its original position or condition.
    
- **Example**: _I had to put the project back on track after the delay._
    

### **26. Take on**

- **Meaning**: To accept responsibility or a challenge.
    
- **Example**: _He took on the role of managing the new department._
    

### **27. Set in**

- **Meaning**: To begin or start to affect something, often referring to a change in the environment or situation.
    
- **Example**: _The cold weather set in early this year._
    

### **28. Get back to**

- **Meaning**: To return to a previous state or task after a change.
    
- **Example**: _We’ll get back to work after lunch._
    

### **29. Change around**

- **Meaning**: To rearrange or change the position of things.
    
- **Example**: _We decided to change the furniture around to make the room more spacious._
    

### **30. Shake up**

- **Meaning**: To make significant changes to something, often to improve it.
    
- **Example**: _The manager decided to shake up the team and restructure the department._
    

### **31. Make over**

- **Meaning**: To change or improve the appearance of something or someone.
    
- **Example**: _The company made over its logo to attract a younger audience._
    

### **32. Take over**

- **Meaning**: To gain control of something, such as a business or process.
    
- **Example**: _The larger company took over the smaller one last month._
    

### **33. Go through**

- **Meaning**: To experience or undergo something, often a difficult process.
    
- **Example**: _They went through many challenges before achieving success._
    

### **34. Move on**

- **Meaning**: To progress or advance from something, often after a significant change.
    
- **Example**: _It’s time to move on from the past and focus on the future._
    

### **35. Catch on**

- **Meaning**: To become popular or accepted after a period of time.
    
- **Example**: _The new trend caught on quickly and spread across the country._
    

### **36. Pull through**

- **Meaning**: To survive or overcome something, especially a difficult or serious situation.
    
- **Example**: _Despite the tough conditions, the team pulled through and finished the project._
    

### **37. Burn out**

- **Meaning**: To exhaust oneself due to overwork or stress.
    
- **Example**: _She was close to burning out after working nonstop for weeks._
    

### **38. Pick up**

- **Meaning**: To improve or increase after a decline.
    
- **Example**: _Sales have picked up after the release of the new product._
    

### **39. Move forward**

- **Meaning**: To progress or continue making changes.
    
- **Example**: _We need to move forward with the plan after the adjustments._
    

### **40. Take off**

- **Meaning**: To become very successful or popular quickly.
    
- **Example**: _Her career took off after she joined the new company._
    

### **41. Die down**

- **Meaning**: To lessen or reduce in intensity.
    
- **Example**: _The noise from the street finally died down after midnight._
    

### **42. Slow down**

- **Meaning**: To reduce speed or the pace of something.
    
- **Example**: _The project slowed down after the budget cuts were announced._
    

### **43. Cut back**

- **Meaning**: To reduce the amount or frequency of something.
    
- **Example**: _The company decided to cut back on unnecessary expenses._
    

### **44. Build up**

- **Meaning**: To accumulate or increase in strength, size, or intensity.
    
- **Example**: _He’s been building up his skills for months._
    

### **45. Phase in**

### **46. Die out**

### **47. Go up**

- **Example**: _The cost of living has gone up significantly this year._
    

### **48. Build up to**

- **Example**: _The event built up to a dramatic conclusion._
    

### **49. Put forward**

- **Meaning**: To suggest or propose an idea or solution.
    
- **Example**: _He put forward an idea for a new marketing campaign._
    

### **50. Wipe out**

- **Example**: _The hurricane wiped out the coastal town._
