Here’s a **comprehensive list of 50 most popular phrasal verbs** related to **time and scheduling**, sorted by popularity, with examples of usage:

### **1. Set up**

- **Meaning**: To arrange or organize something.
    
- **Example**: _We need to set up a meeting for next Thursday._
    

### **2. Plan out**

- **Meaning**: To organize or schedule something in detail.
    
- **Example**: _We should plan out the whole project before starting._
    

### **3. Run into**

- **Meaning**: To encounter something unexpectedly, usually a problem or delay.
    
- **Example**: _We ran into some issues that delayed our progress._
    

### **4. Put off**

- **Meaning**: To postpone or delay something.
    
- **Example**: _We had to put off the meeting because of the weather._
    

### **5. Bring forward**

- **Meaning**: To move an event or meeting to an earlier time.
    
- **Example**: _We decided to bring forward the deadline to next week._
    

### **6. Call off**

- **Meaning**: To cancel an event or meeting.
    
- **Example**: _The meeting was called off due to the manager’s illness._
    

### **7. Take up**

- **Meaning**: To begin or engage in something.
    
- **Example**: _She took up the new project immediately._
    

### **8. Hold off**

- **Meaning**: To delay or postpone an action.
    
- **Example**: _Let’s hold off on making a decision until we get more information._
    

### **9. Check in**

- **Example**: _Please check in with me after the meeting._
    

### **10. Check out**

### **11. Run over**
