Here's a comprehensive list of **50 popular phrasal verbs related to movement and travel**, sorted by popularity, with examples of usage:

---

### **1. Go on**

- **Meaning**: To continue.
    
- **Example**: _We will go on with the tour despite the rain._
    

### **2. Come in**

- **Meaning**: To enter.
    
- **Example**: _Please come in and have a seat._
    

### **3. Set off**

- **Meaning**: To start a journey.
    
- **Example**: _We set off early to avoid traffic._
    

### **4. Get off**

- **Meaning**: To leave a vehicle, bus, train, etc.
    
- **Example**: _I’ll get off at the next station._
    

### **5. Get on**

- **Meaning**: To board a vehicle.
    
- **Example**: _Hurry up and get on the bus!_
    

### **6. Take off**

- **Meaning**: For an aircraft to leave the ground.
    
- **Example**: _The plane will take off in a few minutes._
    

### **7. Drop off**

- **Meaning**: To take someone to a destination and leave them there.
    
- **Example**: _I’ll drop you off at the airport on my way._
    

### **8. Pick up**

- **Meaning**: To collect someone or something.
    
- **Example**: _I’ll pick you up at the hotel at 7 PM._
    

### **9. Turn around**

- **Meaning**: To change direction and go back.
    
- **Example**: _I realized I forgot my wallet, so I had to turn around._
    

### **10. Check in**

- **Meaning**: To register or report upon arrival.
    
- **Example**: _You need to check in at the front desk when you arrive at the hotel._
    

### **11. Check out**

- **Meaning**: To leave a hotel or check something.
    
- **Example**: _We need to check out of the hotel by noon._
    

### **12. Look around**

- **Meaning**: To explore or observe the surroundings.
    
- **Example**: _Let’s look around the city before we head back to the hotel._
    

### **13. Run into**

- **Meaning**: To encounter someone unexpectedly.
    
- **Example**: _I ran into an old friend at the airport yesterday._
    

### **14. Head for**

- **Meaning**: To move towards a destination.
    
- **Example**: _We’re heading for the beach this weekend._
    

### **15. Head back**

- **Meaning**: To return to a previous location.
    
- **Example**: _It’s getting late, we should head back to the hotel._
    

### **16. Get away**

### **18. Stop over**
