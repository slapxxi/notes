Here’s a comprehensive list of **50 popular phrasal verbs related to communication**, sorted by popularity, with examples of usage:

---

### **1. Speak up**

- **Meaning**: To talk louder or express your opinion.
- **Example**: _Could you speak up? I can't hear you very well._

### **2. Bring up**

- **Meaning**: To introduce a topic or subject in a conversation.
- **Example**: _She brought up an interesting point during the meeting._

### **3. Shut up**

- **Meaning**: To stop talking (often used as an imperative).
- **Example**: _He told me to shut up during the argument._

### **4. Call off**

- **Meaning**: To cancel something.
- **Example**: _They called off the meeting because of the snowstorm._

### **5. Get across**

- **Meaning**: To communicate an idea or message clearly.
- **Example**: _She had difficulty getting her point across to the group._

### **6. Talk over**

- **Meaning**: To discuss or debate something.
- **Example**: _Let’s talk over the details of the plan before we make a decision._

### **7. Bring up**

- **Meaning**: To mention or start talking about a topic.
- **Example**: _He brought up the issue of budgets during the presentation._

### **8. Run by**

- **Meaning**: To explain or discuss something with someone to get their opinion.
- **Example**: _Let me run this idea by my boss before we proceed._

### **9. Get through**

- **Meaning**: To successfully communicate or complete something.
- **Example**: _I couldn't get through to her on the phone._

### **10. Talk down to**

- **Meaning**: To speak to someone in a condescending manner.
- **Example**: _She always talks down to me like I'm a child._

### **11. Hold on**

- **Meaning**: To wait (on the phone or in conversation).
- **Example**: _Hold on, let me check my calendar before I give you an answer._

### **12. Break down**

- **Meaning**: To explain something in simple terms.
- **Example**: _Can you break down the concept for me again?_

### **13. Set up**

- **Meaning**: To arrange or organize something.
- **Example**: _I’ll set up a meeting with the team to discuss the project._

### **14. Speak out**

- **Meaning**: To express your opinion publicly, especially on an important issue.
- **Example**: _She spoke out against the new policy at the protest._

### **15. Put forward**

- **Meaning**: To suggest or propose an idea.
- **Example**: _He put forward a new proposal during the meeting._

### **16. Go over**

- **Meaning**: To review or examine something in detail.
- **Example**: _Let’s go over the agenda before the meeting starts._

### **17. Turn down**

- **Meaning**: To reject or refuse an offer or suggestion.
- **Example**: _I had to turn down their invitation to the party._

### **18. Follow up**

- **Meaning**: To contact someone after a previous conversation or event.
- **Example**: _I’ll follow up with an email after the meeting._

### **19. Pick up**

- **Meaning**: To learn or improve on something, especially by informal communication.
- **Example**: _I picked up some useful tips at the workshop._

### **20. Speak for**

- **Meaning**: To represent or express the opinions of someone or a group.
- **Example**: _I can speak for the whole team when I say we support this idea._

### **21. Get back to**

- **Meaning**: To return to someone or something after a delay.
- **Example**: _I’ll get back to you once I have more information._

### **22. Chime in**

- **Meaning**: To join in or interrupt a conversation.
- **Example**: _He chimed in with his opinion during the discussion._

### **23. Follow through**

- **Meaning**: To complete something that was started or promised.
- **Example**: _You need to follow through on your commitment to help with the event._

### **24. Speak up for**

- **Meaning**: To support or defend someone or something in a conversation.
- **Example**: _She spoke up for the workers during the negotiation._

### **25. Ask out**

- **Meaning**: To invite someone on a date.
- **Example**: _He asked her out to dinner last night._

### **26. Fill in**

- **Meaning**: To provide missing information or complete something.
- **Example**: _Can you fill in the details of the report?_

### **27. Take over**

- **Meaning**: To assume control or responsibility for something.
- **Example**: _I’ll take over the presentation if you need a break._

### **28. Call back**

- **Meaning**: To return a phone call.
- **Example**: _I’ll call you back once the meeting is over._

### **29. Wind up**

- **Meaning**: To finish or conclude something.
- **Example**: _We need to wind up this discussion and make a decision._

### **30. Ask around**

- **Meaning**: To inquire with several people.
- **Example**: _I asked around to see if anyone had seen the missing documents._

### **31. Put across**

- **Meaning**: To communicate an idea or message successfully.
- **Example**: _She put across her ideas clearly during the presentation._

### **32. Talk into**

- **Meaning**: To persuade someone to do something.
- **Example**: _He talked me into going to the concert with him._

### **33. Bring about**

- **Meaning**: To cause something to happen.
- **Example**: _His actions brought about a major change in the policy._

### **34. Break up**

- **Meaning**: To end a relationship or an event.
- **Example**: _The band decided to break up after ten years together._

### **35. Settle down**

- **Meaning**: To become calm, or to start a more stable or permanent lifestyle.
- **Example**: _After traveling for years, they decided to settle down in the countryside._

### **36. Talk back**

- **Meaning**: To respond in a rude or disrespectful way.
- **Example**: _She didn’t like it when he talked back during the meeting._

### **37. Look into**

- **Meaning**: To investigate or research something.
- **Example**: _I’ll look into the problem and get back to you._

### **38. Check in**

- **Meaning**: To report or register (for travel or at a hotel).
- **Example**: _I need to check in at the front desk before we start the meeting._

### **39. Check out**

- **Meaning**: To examine or investigate something.
- **Example**: _You should check out that new restaurant in town._

### **40. Point out**

- **Meaning**: To indicate or highlight something.
- **Example**: _She pointed out the key differences between the two proposals._

### **41. Run into**

- **Meaning**: To meet someone unexpectedly.
- **Example**: _I ran into my old teacher at the supermarket._

### **42. Cut off**

- **Meaning**: To interrupt or stop someone from speaking.
- **Example**: _He was cut off while trying to explain his idea._

### **43. Go on about**

- **Meaning**: To talk continuously about something, often in a way that is boring.
- **Example**: _She went on about her vacation for hours._

### **44. Call out**

- **Meaning**: To shout or make a loud noise.
- **Example**: _She called out for help when she couldn’t find her keys._

### **45. Run through**

- **Meaning**: To quickly review or explain something.
- **Example**: _Let’s run through the agenda before the meeting starts._

### **46. Cut in**

- **Meaning**: To interrupt a conversation.
- **Example**: _Sorry to cut in, but I have an important point to add._

### **47. Turn down**

- **Meaning**: To refuse or reject something.
- **Example**: _He turned down the job offer because of the salary._

### **48. Come across**

- **Meaning**: To be understood or interpreted in a particular way.
- **Example**: _She came across as rude, even though she didn’t mean to be._

### **49. Ask for**

- **Meaning**: To request something.
- **Example**: _I asked for a raise during my review._

### **50. Lay out**

- **Meaning**: To explain or present something clearly.
- **Example**: _He laid out his plan for the new project in the meeting._
