Here’s a comprehensive list of **50 popular phrasal verbs related to work and business**, sorted by popularity, with examples of usage:

---

### **1. Set up**

- **Meaning**: To establish or create something (e.g., a business, system, or meeting).
- **Example**: _We need to set up a new meeting room for the conference tomorrow._

### **2. Bring up**

- **Meaning**: To introduce a topic or issue in a conversation.
- **Example**: _She brought up the issue of overtime during the meeting._

### **3. Take over**

- **Meaning**: To assume control or responsibility for something.
- **Example**: _He’s going to take over the marketing department next month._

### **4. Carry out**

- **Meaning**: To perform or complete a task or project.
- **Example**: _The team carried out the research project efficiently._

### **5. Look into**

- **Meaning**: To investigate or examine something.
- **Example**: _I’ll look into the client’s concerns and get back to you._

### **6. Work out**

- **Meaning**: To solve or resolve a problem or situation.
- **Example**: _We need to work out the logistics before the launch._

### **7. Call off**

- **Meaning**: To cancel or postpone something.
- **Example**: _They had to call off the conference due to unforeseen circumstances._

### **8. Follow up**

- **Meaning**: To check or do something after a previous action, to ensure continuity.
- **Example**: _I’ll follow up with you next week to discuss your progress._

### **9. Turn down**

- **Meaning**: To reject an offer or request.
- **Example**: _He turned down the job offer because of the salary._

### **10. Set aside**

- **Meaning**: To reserve or allocate time, money, or resources for a particular purpose.
- **Example**: _We need to set aside some funds for the new project._

### **11. Go over**

- **Meaning**: To review or check something carefully.
- **Example**: _Let’s go over the presentation before the meeting._

### **12. Come up with**

- **Meaning**: To create or think of an idea, plan, or solution.
- **Example**: _We need to come up with a new strategy for increasing sales._

### **13. Run into**

- **Meaning**: To meet or encounter someone unexpectedly.
- **Example**: _I ran into our new client at the conference yesterday._

### **14. Take on**

- **Meaning**: To accept responsibility for something or someone.
- **Example**: _She’s taking on more responsibilities this quarter._

### **15. Break down**

- **Meaning**: To divide something into smaller parts or categories.
- **Example**: _We need to break down the report into key sections._

### **16. Put off**

- **Meaning**: To delay or postpone something.
- **Example**: _The meeting was put off due to scheduling conflicts._

### **17. Look after**

- **Meaning**: To take care of someone or something.
- **Example**: _She looks after the company’s financial records._

### **18. Follow through**

- **Meaning**: To complete or finish a task or project.
- **Example**: _It’s important to follow through with all customer inquiries._

### **19. Run out of**

- **Meaning**: To have no more of something.
- **Example**: _We ran out of office supplies, so we need to reorder them._

### **20. Lay off**

- **Meaning**: To dismiss or let go of an employee, usually due to economic reasons.
- **Example**: _The company had to lay off several workers because of budget cuts._

### **21. Bring in**

- **Meaning**: To introduce or recruit someone or something.
- **Example**: _We’ll bring in a consultant to help improve our operations._

### **22. Settle in**

- **Meaning**: To adjust or get comfortable in a new job or environment.
- **Example**: _It took me a few weeks to settle in at my new job._

### **23. Look forward to**

- **Meaning**: To be excited or eager about something that will happen.
- **Example**: _I’m looking forward to the business trip next week._

### **24. Put up with**

- **Meaning**: To tolerate or endure something unpleasant.
- **Example**: _I don’t know how she puts up with all the office drama._

### **25. Cut down on**

- **Meaning**: To reduce or decrease something, such as costs or time.
- **Example**: _We need to cut down on unnecessary expenses._

### **26. Hand in**

- **Meaning**: To submit something, such as a report or assignment.
- **Example**: _Please hand in your proposals by the end of the day._

### **27. Carry on**

- **Meaning**: To continue doing something.
- **Example**: _Despite the setbacks, we carried on with the project._

### **28. Take off**

- **Meaning**: To become successful or to rise rapidly in a business.
- **Example**: _The startup took off after securing its first big client._

### **29. Work out**

- **Meaning**: To have a successful outcome, especially after effort or planning.
- **Example**: _Our new marketing strategy is working out well._

### **30. Give in**

- **Meaning**: To yield or surrender to pressure or a request.
- **Example**: _After much negotiation, they finally gave in to our terms._

### **31. Get ahead**

- **Meaning**: To advance or progress in one’s career.
- **Example**: _She’s always looking for ways to get ahead in her field._

### **32. Take down**

- **Meaning**: To write or record information.
- **Example**: _I’ll take down the minutes of the meeting for you._

### **33. Deal with**

- **Meaning**: To handle or address a situation or problem.
- **Example**: _I need to deal with a customer complaint before the end of the day._

### **34. Look out for**

- **Meaning**: To be cautious or vigilant about something.
- **Example**: _Please look out for any issues during the product launch._

### **35. Sign off**

- **Meaning**: To approve or finalize something.
- **Example**: _The manager needs to sign off on the budget proposal._

### **36. Back up**

- **Meaning**: To support someone or something, or to make a copy of data.
- **Example**: _Please back up all the important files before the system upgrade._

### **37. Step up**

- **Meaning**: To increase one’s effort or take on a larger role.
- **Example**: _We need to step up our efforts to meet the deadline._

### **38. Turn up**

- **Meaning**: To arrive or appear, especially at a meeting or event.
- **Example**: _He didn’t turn up for the scheduled interview._

### **39. Get by**

- **Meaning**: To manage or survive in difficult circumstances.
- **Example**: _We’re getting by with the limited resources we have._

### **40. Go through**

- **Meaning**: To review or examine something carefully.
- **Example**: _I need to go through these contracts before signing them._

### **41. Check in**

- **Meaning**: To report or provide an update, usually for a meeting or travel.
- **Example**: _Please check in with the office after your meeting._

### **42. Take back**

- **Meaning**: To retract or withdraw something (like a statement or offer).
- **Example**: _I had to take back my comment after realizing it was incorrect._

### **43. Turn in**

- **Meaning**: To submit something, especially work or assignments.
- **Example**: _Don’t forget to turn in your expense reports by the end of the week._

### **44. Hold off**

- **Meaning**: To delay or postpone an action.
- **Example**: _We decided to hold off on the product launch until next month._

### **45. Drop off**

- **Meaning**: To deliver or leave something at a location.
- **Example**: _I’ll drop off the documents at your office tomorrow._

### **46. Brush up on**

- **Meaning**: To review or refresh knowledge of something.
- **Example**: _I need to brush up on my presentation skills for the upcoming meeting._

### **47. Call in**

- **Meaning**: To request the presence or assistance of someone, often over the phone.
- **Example**: _We had to call in an expert to resolve the technical issue._

### **48. Get ahead of**

- **Meaning**: To move forward or make progress faster than others.
- **Example**: _We need to get ahead of our competitors to secure the deal._

### **49. Take up**

- **Meaning**: To start or begin something, such as a new task or responsibility.
- **Example**: _He’s taking up the responsibility of managing the new team._

### **50. Bring about**

- **Meaning**: To cause something to happen.
- **Example**: _The new policies will bring about significant changes in the company._
