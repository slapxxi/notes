### **Comprehensive List of Adverbial Clauses of Contrast with Examples**

Adverbial clauses of contrast describe how two ideas, actions, or situations are different. They help to show the relationship between contrasting actions, facts, or circumstances. These clauses answer the question **"How does one action differ from another?"**

---

## **1. Common Subordinating Conjunctions for Contrast**

These conjunctions are used to introduce adverbial clauses that express contrast:

|**Conjunction**|**Example Sentence**|
|---|---|
|**Although**|_Although it was raining, she went for a walk._|
|**Though**|_Though he is tired, he will finish the work._|
|**Even though**|_Even though she studied hard, she didn’t pass the exam._|
|**Whereas**|_She likes tea, whereas he prefers coffee._|
|**While**|_While I enjoy hiking, my brother prefers cycling._|
|**But**|_I wanted to go to the park, but it started to rain._|
|**In spite of the fact that**|_In spite of the fact that he was late, he managed to finish the task on time._|
|**Despite the fact that**|_Despite the fact that she is new to the company, she understands everything quickly._|
|**On the other hand**|_I enjoy reading books; on the other hand, my friend loves watching movies._|
|**However**|_The weather was terrible; however, they decided to go on the trip._|

## **3. How to Use Adverbial Clauses of Contrast in Speech**

### **A. Comparing Two Actions**

- **"Although he studied hard, he didn’t get the promotion."**
    
- **"She went to the gym even though she was feeling sick."**
    

### **B. Contrasting Ideas or Statements**

- **"He prefers to travel by car, whereas I like to fly."**
    
- **"I love working in the office, while my colleague prefers to work from home."**
    

### **C. Describing Situations that Differ**

- **"In spite of the fact that it was raining, they decided to go for a run."**
    
- **"Despite the fact that she is young, she is very knowledgeable."**
