### **Extensive List of Adverbial Clauses of Time**

**Adverbial clauses of time** describe **when** an action happens. They provide additional information about the timing of the main clause and are introduced by subordinating conjunctions such as _when, while, before, after, as soon as, until,_ and more.

---

## **1. Common Subordinating Conjunctions for Time Clauses**

| **Conjunction**          | **Usage**                                                          | **Example Sentence**                                          |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **When**                 | Indicates a specific moment in time                                | _She called me **when** she arrived._                         |
| **While**                | Describes two simultaneous actions                                 | _I was cooking **while** he was setting the table._           |
| **Before**               | Describes an action that happens earlier than another              | _Finish your homework **before** you go outside._             |
| **After**                | Describes an action that happens later than another                | _We went home **after** the movie ended._                     |
| **As soon as**           | Emphasizes immediate sequence                                      | _Call me **as soon as** you get home._                        |
| **Until** / **Till**     | Shows the limit of an action                                       | _He waited **until** she arrived._                            |
| **Since**                | Indicates the starting point of an action that continues           | _I have been here **since** Monday._                          |
| **As**                   | Indicates simultaneous or progressive action                       | _I listened to music **as** I worked._                        |
| **By the time**          | Expresses something that happens before another event is completed | _By the time we arrived, the show had already started._       |
| **Whenever**             | Refers to any time something happens                               | _You can visit me **whenever** you want._                     |
| **The moment**           | Emphasizes an exact point in time                                  | _She left **the moment** she heard the news._                 |
| **Every time**           | Describes a repeated action                                        | _He smiles **every time** he sees her._                       |
| **No sooner... than...** | Describes an action that happens immediately after another         | _No sooner had I sat down **than** the phone rang._           |
| **Hardly... when...**    | Similar to "No sooner... than..."                                  | _I had **hardly** left home **when** it started raining._     |
| **Scarcely... when...**  | Similar to "No sooner... than..." but slightly more formal         | _She had **scarcely** stepped inside **when** the bell rang._ |

---

## **2. Example Sentences for Each Type**

### **A. "When" Clauses (Specific Moments in Time)**

1. _I was sleeping **when** you called me._
2. _He felt nervous **when** he stepped onto the stage._
3. _We will go shopping **when** the rain stops._

### **B. "While" Clauses (Simultaneous Actions)**

4. _She listened to music **while** she was working._
5. _I was driving **while** talking on the phone._
6. _They were laughing **while** watching the comedy show._

### **C. "Before" Clauses (Earlier Action)**

7. _She had already left **before** I arrived._
8. _Close the door **before** you go out._
9. _We need to finish this project **before** the deadline._

### **D. "After" Clauses (Later Action)**

10. _We’ll have dinner **after** the meeting._
11. _The children went to bed **after** they brushed their teeth._
12. _I will call you **after** I finish my work._

### **E. "As Soon As" Clauses (Immediate Actions)**

13. _She called me **as soon as** she got home._
14. _The dog ran to me **as soon as** it saw me._
15. _I will text you **as soon as** I hear the news._

### **F. "Until / Till" Clauses (Action Limits)**

16. _Stay here **until** I return._
17. _He didn’t stop working **until** midnight._
18. _We waited **till** the store opened._

### **G. "Since" Clauses (Starting Point in Time)**

19. _I have been working here **since** 2015._
20. _She hasn’t seen him **since** he moved away._
21. _We have been best friends **since** childhood._

### **H. "As" Clauses (Simultaneous or Progressive Actions)**

22. _I watched **as** the sun set behind the mountains._
23. _He improved his skills **as** he practiced more._
24. _She smiled **as** she read the letter._

### **I. "By the Time" Clauses (Action Completed Before Another)**

25. _By the time we arrived, the train had already left._
26. _She had finished her work **by the time** we got there._
27. _He was asleep **by the time** the movie ended._

### **J. "Whenever" Clauses (Any Time Something Happens)**

28. _Come visit me **whenever** you’re free._
29. _He gets nervous **whenever** he speaks in public._
30. _The baby cries **whenever** she is hungry._

### **K. "The Moment" Clauses (Exact Point in Time)**

31. _She left **the moment** she finished her speech._
32. _Call me **the moment** you arrive._
33. _The dog ran outside **the moment** the door was opened._

### **L. "Every Time" Clauses (Repeated Actions)**

34. _I feel happy **every time** I see you._
35. _She laughs **every time** I tell that joke._
36. _The lights flicker **every time** there’s a storm._

### **M. "No Sooner... Than..." Clauses (Immediate Sequence)**

37. _No sooner had I closed my eyes **than** the alarm rang._
38. _No sooner did they sit down **than** the waiter arrived._
39. _No sooner had he left **than** it started raining._

### **N. "Hardly... When..." Clauses (Immediate Sequence)**

40. _I had **hardly** stepped outside **when** it began to rain._
41. _He had **hardly** finished eating **when** the phone rang._
42. _She had **hardly** spoken **when** she was interrupted._

### **O. "Scarcely... When..." Clauses (Immediate Sequence, Formal)**

43. _I had **scarcely** sat down **when** the meeting started._
44. _She had **scarcely** finished reading **when** she fell asleep._
45. _We had **scarcely** left the house **when** it started to snow._

### **P. "Rather... Than..." (Preference in Time Context)**

46. _He left early **rather** than wait for the bus._
47. _She chose to walk **rather** than take a taxi._
48. _I prefer to study **rather** than go to a party._

### **Q. "The More... The More..." (Proportional Relationship)**

49. _The **more** I practice, **the better** I get._
50. _The **more** you sleep, **the more** refreshed you feel._

---

## **How to Incorporate Adverbial Clauses of Time into Speech**

1. **Practice combining sentences**: Instead of saying _I finished my work. Then I went home._, say _I went home **after** I finished my work._
2. **Use them in daily conversation**: When telling a story, add time clauses naturally (e.g., _I was cooking **when** my phone rang._)
3. **Use them in professional settings**: In interviews or work situations, use precise time expressions (_By the time we launched, the product was perfect._)
