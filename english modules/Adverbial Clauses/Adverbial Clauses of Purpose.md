### **Comprehensive List of Adverbial Clauses of Purpose with Examples**

Adverbial clauses of **purpose** describe the **reason** or **intention** behind an action. They explain **why** something is done. These clauses are introduced by subordinating conjunctions that indicate the purpose of the main action. They answer the question **"Why?"** or **"For what purpose?"**

---

## **1. Common Subordinating Conjunctions for Purpose**

These conjunctions are used to introduce adverbial clauses of purpose:

|**Conjunction**|**Example Sentence**|
|---|---|
|**So that**|_She worked hard **so that** she could afford a vacation._|
|**In order that**|_They saved money **in order that** they could buy a new house._|
|**So**|_He woke up early **so** he could catch the bus._|
|**For the purpose of**|_She went to the store **for the purpose of** buying groceries._|
|**To**|_He studied hard **to** pass the exam._|

## **3. How to Use Adverbial Clauses of Purpose in Speech**

### **A. Explaining Reasons for Actions**

- **"She worked extra hours **so that** she could save for a new car."**
    
- **"I study every day **so** I can improve my skills."**
    

### **B. Expressing Intentions**

- **"He moved to the city **in order that** he could pursue his career."**
    
- **"They planned the event **for the purpose of** raising funds for charity."**
    

### **C. Giving Explanations**

- **"She woke up early **so that** she could go for a run before work."**
    
- **"He joined the team **to** learn more about the project."**
