### **Comprehensive List of Adverbial Clauses of Degree/Extent with Examples**

Adverbial clauses of **degree/extent** describe **how much** or **to what degree** an action occurs or a situation is true. These clauses answer the questions **"How much?"** or **"To what extent?"** and are often introduced by subordinating conjunctions that express degree or intensity.

---

## **1. Common Subordinating Conjunctions for Degree/Extent**

These conjunctions are used to introduce adverbial clauses of degree/extent:

| **Conjunction**          | **Example Sentence**                                                     |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **As**                   | _She is **as** tall **as** her brother._                                 |
| **So**                   | _He is **so** tired **that** he can barely keep his eyes open._          |
| **Than**                 | _This book is better **than** that one._                                 |
| **As... as**             | _This test is **as** difficult **as** the last one._                     |
| **The more... the more** | _**The more** I study, **the more** confident I feel._                   |
| **Even though**          | _He was very successful, **even though** his journey was difficult._     |
| **To the extent that**   | _She was **to the extent that** she could hardly breathe from laughing._ |

## **2. Example Sentences with Adverbial Clauses of Degree/Extent**

| **Even though**          | _She completed the task **even though** it was time-consuming._      |
| **To the extent that**   | _The storm was severe **to the extent that** the roads were closed._ |

---

## **3. How to Use Adverbial Clauses of Degree/Extent in Speech**

### **A. Describing Comparisons**

- **"This city is **as** beautiful **as** Paris."**
- **"His performance was better **than** I expected."**

### **B. Expressing the Intensity of a Situation**

- **"She was **so** upset **that** she started crying."**
- **"The news was **so** shocking **that** no one could speak."**

### **C. Talking About Changes and Gradients**

- **"The more I learn, **the more** I realize how much there is to know."**
- **"The more he practiced, **the better** his skills became."**
